Adobong Manok

A popular Filipino national dish consisting of chicken pieces—usually thighs and drumsticks—braised in a savory, tangy mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns

Ingredients

  • Chicken: 2 lbs (about 1 kg) of thighs or drumsticks for the best flavor.
  • Garlic: 1 head (or at least 5–6 cloves), crushed.
  • Soy Sauce: ½ cup (preferably a Filipino brand for authenticity).
  • Vinegar: ⅓ to ½ cup (cane or white distilled vinegar).

Instructions

  1. Marinate: In a large bowl, combine the chicken, soy sauce, and crushed garlic. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes (up to overnight) to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat.
  2. Sear: Heat a small amount of oil in a pot over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade (reserve the liquid) and fry the pieces until they are golden brown on all sides.
  3. Simmer: Pour the reserved marinade and about 1 cup of water into the pot. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat to a simmer. Cover and cook for 20–30 minutes until the chicken is tender.
  4. Add Vinegar: Pour in the vinegar. Crucial tip: Do not stir the pot for the first 2–3 minutes after adding vinegar to let the "raw" acidity cook off.
